Symptom analysis: Which type of STD do you belong to?

Symptom 1: A single round or oval nodule of about 1cm appears in the glans area, with a hardness like rubber or cartilage, without conscious pain or tenderness. Ulceration is formed when it intensifies in the later stage. The incubation period is 2 to 3 weeks.

Initial diagnosis: syphilis

Symptom 2: Small and itchy red papules appeared on the genitals, which quickly turned into small blisters after a few days, and soon formed pustules. After ulceration, erosions, ulcers, and scabs formed, accompanied by pain. The incubation period is 2 to 7 days.

Initial diagnosis: genital herpes

Symptom 3: mostly occurs in the coronal sulcus of the glans penis. It is a skin-colored or light red needle-capped large soft papule with a smooth surface, arranged in one or two rows along the coronal sulcus. The incubation period is 3 weeks to 8 months.

Preliminary diagnosis: genital warts

Symptom 4: The urethral opening is red, swollen and itchy, accompanied by tingling pain, and mucus flows out, which is dark yellow or yellow-green. The urethra often has irritation, accompanied by symptoms such as frequent urination, urgency and dysuria. Incubation period 1 to 14 days。

Preliminary diagnosis: Gonorrhea

Symptom 5: Common symptoms are painful urination and a little mucus or mucopurulent discharge in the urethra, the amount is small, and sometimes you need to squeeze the anterior urethra to find a little discharge. The incubation period is 1 to 3 weeks.
