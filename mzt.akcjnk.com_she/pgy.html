Causes of cystitis

Cystitis is a common urinary tract infectious disease, accounting for about 50% to 70% of the total number of urinary tract infections. It is mainly caused by specific and non-specific bacterial infections, and there are other special types of cystitis. Specific infection refers to bladder tuberculosis. Nonspecific cystitis is caused by Escherichia coli, vice Escherichia coli, Proteus, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, Streptococcus faecalis and Staphylococcus aureus.

Common cystitis treatment mistakes
Myth 1: Medication indiscriminately

There is more than one cause of cystitis. Some patients are used to buying medicine on their own, but this often backfires. It is best to go to the hospital for testing and diagnosis, and then prescribe medicine according to the cause.

Misunderstanding 2: Failure to adhere to treatment

Some patients stop treatment as soon as their symptoms improve. In fact, the improvement of symptoms does not mean cure. Stopping treatment too early will make the remaining pathogenic microorganisms revive.。
